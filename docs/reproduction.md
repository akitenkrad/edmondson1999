# Reproduction

The model maps to Edmondson (1999)'s field-study numbers by treating the simulated team cross-section the way the paper treats its 51-team sample: compute ICC and the Baron & Kenny (1986) three-step mediation on team-level constructs.

## Stable team-level measures

Team learning `L_k` and performance `Π_k` are noisy single-tick rates in small (n_k≈8) teams. The reproduction uses each team's **time-average over the run's second half** as the stable, survey-equivalent measure — exactly the cross-section written to `team_cross_section.csv`. ICC(ψ) is computed on the per-individual ψ at the final tick; ICC(L) on each individual's time-averaged voice rate grouped by team.

## Per-trial averaging

`reproduce` runs `--runs` independent trials and computes the anchors on **each** trial's ~90-team cross-section, then averages the statistics. This preserves the paper's n≈51 statistical power; pooling all trials into one regression would inflate power so far that even the mediated residual (which should be non-significant) turns "significant".

## Rust vs Python

- `cargo run --release -- reproduce` prints the anchors using the local OLS / ICC / Baron & Kenny implementations in `metrics.rs`.
- `uv run edmondson-tools reproduce` reads `team_cross_section.csv` and recomputes the three-step mediation per trial with **statsmodels** (exact-t p-values), adds a **bootstrap** of the indirect effect a·b with a 95% bias-corrected percentile CI, runs the efficacy discriminant `L ~ ψ + efficacy`, and writes `table4_report.csv` + `mediation_bootstrap.csv`. The two paths agree on the anchor verdicts.

## H5 / H8 (efficacy discriminant)

Team efficacy η_k evolves toward a per-team **independent** latent target (not ψ, not performance). Controlling for ψ, efficacy carries little information about learning behavior, so its partial |t| falls below 2 — reproducing the paper's finding that efficacy does **not** explain learning once psychological safety is held constant (H5 and H8 unsupported). A correct replication therefore leaves H5/H8 out of the supported-hypothesis tally, landing 6 of 8.
